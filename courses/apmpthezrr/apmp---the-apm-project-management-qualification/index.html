Aims

The APMP is the professional qualification awarded by the Association for Project Management. The focus of this intensive course is to provide you with a level of knowledge that will enhance your personal effectiveness in project management and give you a professional qualification that is recognised worldwide.

Objectives

The syllabus is based upon the current Body of Knowledge (BoK) and the sessions will include discussions, exercises, case studies and example questions.

Given the intensive nature of the syllabus the course will involve at least two hours of study each evening. To facilitate this you may wish to consider our accommodation package offering reduced rates at local hotels.

The examination consists of a three-hour essay style paper.

APMP is a knowledge based qualification. Successful candidates are able to participate in projects from individual assignments through to large capital projects. APMP is a qualification recognised both nationally and internationally that successful candidates can carry from one job to another or from one industry to another.

APMP covers 12 high level Learning Outcomes from the APM Body of Knowledge. Awareness of these is considered fundamental to the professional management of projects.

The course is fully accredited and approved by the APM, and Hemsley Fraser is an APM Accredited Training Provider.

Is it right for me?

Suitable for people managing projects in any industry already using some formal project management processes and methods who wish to complete the APMP examination via an intensive five-day programme.

What will I learn?

By the end of this course you will be able to:

  • Provide organisations with a proven project management methodology that can allow people to plan, organise and control the successful implementation of projects.
  • Achieve the APMP qualification, the UK’s leading project management qualification.
  • Provide practical skills and techniques to help further your career in project management.

Summary

Pre-course Activity

Delegates are required to complete approximately 15 to 20 hours of work in advance of the course from the APM Body of Knowledge manual and the Pre-Course Guide, both of which will be posted to delegates prior to the course. Delegates are also advised to download support documentation from the APM website to assist with their study and must complete and return an APM Application Form to the course administrator. This course also requires some structured self-study during the course to cover the material contained within the exam.
