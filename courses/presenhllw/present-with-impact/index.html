Present with Impact

  • Course Type: Class
  • Duration: 1 Day
  • Price: £495 +VAT
  • Share This Course

Available Dates

There are no available dates for this course.

Aims

This is a highly practical event combining the most up-to-date and effective thinking with plenty of practice at various types of presentation. It is especially suitable for those seeking to develop their presentations skills further and focuses not only on the presentation, but also the presenter, delivering effective skills that will enable delegates to engage even more effectively with their audiences.

Video capture will be used to allow delegates to take a copy of their presentations away as an aid to ongoing learning. These will not be ‘shown’ during the workshop. Topics for presentations can include both work and personal subjects and pre-course preparation is required.

Objectives

  • Have greater personal confidence when presenting.
  • Deliver presentations with greater personal impact.
  • Better engage with their audience and be able to overcome ‘difficult’ situations.

Summary

As a result of attending this course, delegates will be able to

  • Have greater personal confidence when presenting.
  • Deliver presentations with greater personal impact.
  • Better engage with their audience and be able to overcome ‘difficult’ situations.


Course Overview

  • Brief overview of delegate needs and objectives for the workshop.
  • Establishing different types of presentations and their objectives.
  • Identifying specific desired outcomes.
  • Exploring different presentation arenas and differing presentation challenges.


Understanding the major faults of presentations and common, reoccurring faults

  • Exploring a sound formula for designing and delivering convincing and effective presentations.


Understanding the needs of the audience both general and individual 

  • Who is attending, what are their views, needs, priorities and decision making processes and timescales.
  • Who is missing and how can we influence them through others?.


Managing the audience, setting expectations and leading their ‘mindset’ to a receptive state

  • Preparing for the worst questions your audience can ask.


Designing – Content, structure, strategy and delivery method. What methods, media, timings, techniques and equipment to use

  • Constructing the message and building the content.
  • Focusing on both big picture and detail.
  • When to be precise and when to be ‘artfully vague’.
  • How to skilfully challenge assumptions that may be barriers and how to reinforce key points and summarise effectively.


Developing techniques for maximising effectiveness and influencing the audience using proven psychology and motivation techniques

  • How to aim your communication at the conscious mind and also the below awareness processing of the audience.
  • ‘Pacing and leading’.
  • The ‘6 sermon’ technique.
  • Storytelling and the power of analogy, metaphor and suggestion.
  • Exploring the whole ‘presentation toolkit’.


Developing your Charisma and Personal Impact

  • What are the elements of Charisma and how can I use them.
  • Awareness of audience responses and ‘state’.
  • How can I use my voice to best effect?.
  • Where and how do I stand?.
  • How much movement should I make, what do I do with my hands?.
  • Controlling those nerves.
  • How can I come over confident when I don’t feel it?.
  • Thinking on your feet and options for reacting to negativity.


Action plans and close

  • Participants commit to a plan of action that they will implement on return to the workplace so that they derive maximum benefit from the training event and a tangible return is realised within the business.
